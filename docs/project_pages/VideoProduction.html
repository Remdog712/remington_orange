Video Production

Projects spanning high school, college, and professional work

One featured example from a larger body of edited, animated, and produced video work.

I have worked on a wide range of video projects stretching from high school through college and beyond, creating work for classes, campus initiatives, research communication, promotional projects, creative experiments, and collaborative storytelling. Across those projects I have handled planning, filming, editing, motion work, and the overall shaping of a video to match its audience and purpose.

Some of these videos were made to explain ideas clearly, others were built as trailers, motion studies, or digital art pieces, and others supported organizations like the VAR Lab through branded intros and travel videos. Together they show how I approach video production as both a technical process and a communication tool, adapting the style, pacing, and structure of each piece to fit what it needs to do.

Range

Work includes educational videos, motion experiments, game trailers, environmental simulations, promotional edits, and archived early productions.

Tools

These projects draw on editing, compositing, pacing, sound choices, thumbnail design, motion graphics, and video formatting for web delivery.

Contexts

The videos were created for coursework, research communication, organization branding, personal experimentation, and collaborative creative work.
